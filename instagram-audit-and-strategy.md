## The Honest Assessment

### What's Working
- **Your photography is genuinely beautiful.** The work speaks for itself when people see it.
- **Your warmest, most personal content performs best.** The birthday self-portrait Reel (Oct 2025) got 114 likes, 49 comments, and 1,839 plays. That's 3-10x your average post performance.
- **You have a loyal community.** Comments like "Best photographer ever" and "Come to Peru, we miss you" show real connection. People love YOU, not just your photos.
- **Wedding and family content from 2019-2022 performed well.** Posts routinely hit 50-127 likes with engaged comments.

### What's Holding You Back

**1. Posting consistency is the biggest issue.**
Your last 30 posts span from 2019 to June 2025. That's roughly 30 posts across 6 years. There are multi-month gaps everywhere. Instagram's algorithm rewards consistency, and dormant accounts get buried.

Timeline of recent activity:
- June 2025: 1 post (comeback post after 7-month gap)
- Oct-Nov 2024: ~5 posts (mini session season burst)
- Oct 2025: 1 Reel (birthday photos)
- Sep 2025: 1 wedding collab post
- Before that: scattered posts through 2022-2023

**2. Almost zero Reels.**
Out of 30 recent posts, only 4-5 are video/Reels. Your ONE personal Reel outperformed everything else by a massive margin. Instagram is pushing Reels hard. You're leaving reach on the table.

**3. Bio needs a total overhaul.**
- No mention of what you photograph (families, weddings, seniors)
- No call to action (book now, link to portfolio)
- No location keyword for search ("Las Vegas" is only in the display name)
- The @jennieslade and @secondchance5k cross-links pull attention away from booking

**4. Hashtag strategy is inconsistent.**
Some posts have 4 hashtags, some have 20, some have zero. There's no consistent hashtag set. Also, several hashtags are misspelled (#jenneisladefamilies, #lasvegasfamilyohotographer).

**5. Content is 95% client deliverables.**
Almost every post is "here are photos from a session." No behind-the-scenes, no tips, no gear talk, no personal stories, no day-in-the-life content. The algorithm and your audience are both hungry for variety.

## Reels Strategy (This Is Where the Growth Lives)

Your birthday Reel proved it. Here's why Reels matter:
- Your Reel got 1,839 plays and 114 likes
- Your average image post gets 30-60 likes
- Reels reach people who DON'T follow you. Images mostly reach people who DO.

**Easy Reels you can make from what you already have:**
1. Photo slideshows set to trending audio (15-30 seconds)
2. Before/after edits (raw vs. final)
3. "Then vs. now" client comparisons
4. Quick behind-the-scenes clips from sessions
5. Golden hour timelapse at your favorite locations
6. Talking to camera about your photography journey
7. "POV: You booked a family session with me" (session highlights)

**You don't need to dance. You don't need to lip sync. You need to show up.**

## What NOT to Do

- Don't buy followers or use engagement pods
- Don't post and ghost. Spend 10-15 minutes after posting replying to comments and engaging with others' posts
- Don't overthink it. A good post today beats a perfect post never
- Don't only post client work. Mix in your personality. Your birthday Reel proved that Jennie-the-person gets more love than Jennie-the-portfolio
- Don't use 20+ hashtags. It looks spammy. Keep it to 10-15 relevant ones

## The Bottom Line

You have the talent, the archive, the story, and the community. What you don't have is consistency and a Reels strategy. Fix those two things and you'll see real movement within 60-90 days.

Your single most powerful asset is your longevity. "I photographed her as a baby, and 18 years later I shot her senior portraits" is the kind of content that goes viral in the photography space. Lead with that story. It's uniquely yours.
