</div><span>Lately, I have a thing for </span><span>tilapia..</span><span><span>. basically an every day thing.  These fish tacos are easy, healthy, tasty, and perfect for summer.  Yum. 

Oh yeah, don't forget the avocado... never forget the avocado.
</span></span><span >
Ingredients</span>
<ul><li>1 pound boneless, skinless tilapia fillets, cut into 2-inch pieces</li><li>2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil</li><li> Coarse salt and ground pepper</li><li> 1/4 cup sour cream</li><li>1 lime, half finely zested and juiced, half cut into wedges</li><li> Hot sauce, such as Tabasco</li><li>12 corn tortillas</li><li> 1/2 small head red cabbage, thinly sliced</li><li>1 cup fresh cilantro</li><li>1 small white onion, finely chopped</li></ul>  <div class="ms-col2-recipe-directions"> <h2><span>Directions</span></h2> <ol><li> <span>Heat broiler, with rack in highest position. Pat fish dry with  paper towels and gently coat with oil on a rimmed baking sheet. Season  with salt and pepper. Broil until fish is browned on top and flesh is  opaque throughout, 5 minutes.</span> </li><li> <span>Meanwhile, in a small bowl, combine sour cream, lime zest and  juice, and a few dashes hot sauce. Season with salt and pepper. Toast  corn tortillas over a kitchen burner using tongs or wrap in  parchment-lined foil and heat in oven, 5 minutes. Divide fish evenly  among tortillas and top with cabbage, cilantro, and onion. Serve with  lime sour cream and lime wedges.</span> </li></ol></div>
