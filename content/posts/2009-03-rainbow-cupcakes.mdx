<div><span >i made these for the kids classes on St. Patrick's Day. (You know... rainbow, pot of gold ,etc.:)</span><span >  super duper easy and fun to make, and the kids thought they were cool, which is a plus.</span>
</div></div>
<ol><li><span>Mix the white cake batter according to package directions.</span></li><li><span>Evenly divide prepared batter into several different bowls. The amount of bowls will depend on the amount of colors you want for your cupcakes.</span></li><li><span>Add a generous amount of gel food coloring to each bowl until you have a vibrant batter.</span></li><li><span>I mixed 5 bowls - red, orange, blue, purple, green
</span></li><li><span>Line your muffin tin with paper cups and then begin spooning the colored batter into the cups.</span></li><li><span>Add batter color by color trying to make an even layer on the bottom... (I got lazy on that part, but they still turned out.)
</span></li><li><span>Fill the cups approximately 2/3 full of batter.</span></li><li><span>Bake as directed on the box.</span></li><li><span>Allow the cupcakes to cool completely, and frost to your liking.</span></li><li><span>I made a cream cheese frosting (because it's my fav) and decorated with rainbow sprinkles.
</span></li></ol><span>*** the plan was to pipe on puffy clouds, but i ran out of time and frosting.  you could also use marshmallows as clouds, or put a gold coin on top etc. etc... you catch the idea.

Enjoy!</span>
