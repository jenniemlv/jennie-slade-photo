My son's a genius.

Let me tell you how I know this:

Jackson has been telling us that all he wants for Christmas is <span>a rope</span>.  He has told us this for a couple months now.  He actually wants two ropes, one with a hook on the end, and one plain rope, so that he can climb tall mountains.  I decided that this can't be ALL he wants for Christmas so I told him to think really hard about something he might want. A couple days later he came up to me and this is how the dialog went:

<span>Jackson:  Mom! I figured out what I want for Christmas, and it's only 1 thing, and its all I want.</span>

<span>Mom:  Jackson, that's great that your were able to think of just 1 thing, what is it?</span>

<span>Jackson:  A TOY MAKER MACHINE!</span>

<span>Mom:  A what?</span>

<span>Jackson:  A TOY MAKER MACHINE! All you have to do is push a button and say what toy you want and you can get it.

Mom:Wow, where do they have "Toy Maker Machines."

Jackson: Santa makes them... remember he can make anything mom?!  And I know I'll get it because I am just asking for 1 thing.

</span>Bet you wish your child was this smart...
