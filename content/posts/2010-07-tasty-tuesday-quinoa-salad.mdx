<div><div>I've decided every Tuesday is going to be "Tasty Tuesday" where I will  post a new favorite recipe, or snack that I am obsessed with at the  moment.  Hopefully I can keep it up.

And here's the first "Tasty  Tuesday" recipe.

I loved this salad with avocado on top, because  everything tastes better with a little avocado on it. I omitted the  butter and added some olive oil.  It was great for dinner with some  steamed tilapia and even better for lunch the next day with some  buckwheat crackers.  This recipe has a whole complex way for making  Quinoa. I don't have time for complexity in my life so I just stick it  in the rice cooker with double the water and it comes out perfect.   Enjoy!

<div><span><span >Quinoa Black Bean Salad</span></span>
</div>

Ingredients
<ul class="ingredientsList"><li class="ingredient">2 teaspoons grated lime  zest</li><li class="ingredient">2 tablespoons fresh lime  juice</li><li class="ingredient">2 tablespoons unsalted  butter, melted and cooled</li><li class="ingredient">1 tablespoon vegetable oil</li><li class="ingredient">1 teaspoon sugar</li><li class="ingredient">1 cup quinoa</li><li class="ingredient">1 (14- to 15-ounce) can  black beans, rinsed and drained</li><li class="ingredient">2 medium tomatoes, diced</li><li class="ingredient">4 scallions, chopped</li><li class="ingredient">1/4 cup chopped fresh  cilantro</li></ul>                    Preparation         <div id="preparation">                                        <p class="instructions">                                  Whisk together lime zest and juice, butter, oil, sugar,  1/2 teaspoon salt, and  1/4teaspoon pepper in a large bowl.             </p>                                   <p class="instructions">                                  Wash quinoa in 3 changes of cold water in a bowl,  draining in a sieve each time.             </p>                                   <p class="instructions">                                  Cook quinoa in a medium pot of boiling salted water (1  tablespoon salt for  2 quarts water), uncovered, until almost tender,  about 10 minutes. Drain in  sieve, then set sieve in same pot with 1  inch of simmering water (water should not touch bottom of sieve). Cover  quinoa with a folded kitchen towel, then cover sieve with a lid (don't  worry if lid doesn't fit tightly) and steam over medium heat until  tender, fluffy, and dry, about 10 minutes. Remove pot from heat and  remove lid. Let stand, still covered with towel, 5 minutes.             </p>                                   <p class="instructions">                                  Add quinoa to dressing and toss until dressing is  absorbed, then  stir in remaining ingredients and salt and pepper to  taste.</p><p class="instructions"><img src="/journal/2010-07-tasty-tuesday-quinoa-salad/0b79eca486.jpg" alt="" /></p><p class="instructions">recipe from <a href="http://www.epicurious.com/recipes/food/views/Black-Bean-and-Tomato-Quinoa-238939">epicurious</a>
</p></div>
