<span>For a new "First Day of School" tradition (thanks Lisa) we went to a </span><span  >new </span><span>restaurant to celebrate all of the <span>new </span>happenings of the day.  I don't know how happy the kids were about eating Thai food, but the parents and grandparents were in heaven.  After dinner, they were spoiled with some </span><a href="http://goldenspoon.com/">Golden Spoon. </a>
</div><div><img src="/journal/2007-08-fresh-start/1322bb74bc.jpg" alt="" /><img src="/journal/2007-08-fresh-start/8b40d39277.jpg" alt="" />

<span>The new school year always gets me excited to re-commit myself:</span>
<div><span>To be a better wife, to be a better mom, to be more organized, to stop worrying about the silly things that don't matter, have meaningful conversations with my husband and children, be a better friend, to make memories, to exercise regularly, feed my family quality food, enjoy each day.</span>
<span  >
</span><div><span  >Like my dad always says, <span>
"Today is the first day of the rest of your life." </span>
It's always nice to start with a clean slate, just like the feeling on the first day of school.</span>
