Last night I made some fish tacos that were "to die for" literally, you could die from eating them.  They are battered, fried fish tacos.... so amazingly good.  This was actually the first time I ever fried anything, but now I have entered the dark side cause these things rocked... I was so busy enjoying the goodness that I didn't bother to take a picture, but I did steal one that looked similar...

<div class="ingredients">         <h3>             Ingredients</h3>                          <ul><li class="plaincharacterwrap">                     1 cup all-purpose flour</li><li class="plaincharacterwrap">                     2 tablespoons cornstarch</li><li class="plaincharacterwrap">                     1 teaspoon baking powder</li><li class="plaincharacterwrap">                     1/2 teaspoon salt</li><li class="plaincharacterwrap">                     1 egg</li><li class="plaincharacterwrap">                     1 cup beer</li><li class="plaincharacterwrap">                     
</li><li class="plaincharacterwrap">                     1/2 cup plain yogurt</li><li class="plaincharacterwrap">                     1/2 cup mayonnaise</li><li class="plaincharacterwrap">                     1 lime, juiced</li><li class="plaincharacterwrap">                     1 jalapeno pepper, minced</li><li class="plaincharacterwrap">                     1 teaspoon minced capers</li><li class="plaincharacterwrap">                     1/2 teaspoon dried oregano</li><li class="plaincharacterwrap">                     1/2 teaspoon ground cumin</li><li class="plaincharacterwrap">                     1/2 teaspoon dried dill weed</li><li class="plaincharacterwrap">                     1 teaspoon ground cayenne pepper</li><li class="plaincharacterwrap">                     
</li><li class="plaincharacterwrap">                     1 quart oil for frying</li><li class="plaincharacterwrap">                     1 pound cod fillets, cut into 2 to 3 ounce portions</li><li class="plaincharacterwrap">                     1 (12 ounce) package corn tortillas</li><li class="plaincharacterwrap">                     1/2 medium head cabbage, finely shredded</li></ul>                  </div>     <div>     </div>     <div class="directions">         <h3>             Directions</h3>                                   <ol><li><span class="plaincharacterwrap break">                     To make beer batter:  In a large bowl, combine  flour, cornstarch, baking powder, and salt. Blend egg and beer, then  quickly stir into the flour mixture (don't worry about a few lumps).                 </span></li><li><span class="plaincharacterwrap break">                     To make white sauce:  In a medium bowl, mix together  yogurt and mayonnaise. Gradually stir in fresh lime juice until  consistency is slightly runny. Season with jalapeno, capers, oregano,  cumin, dill, and cayenne.                 </span></li><li><span class="plaincharacterwrap break">                     Heat oil in deep-fryer to 375 degrees F (190 degrees  C).                 </span></li><li><span class="plaincharacterwrap break">                     Dust fish pieces lightly with flour. Dip into beer  batter, and fry until crisp and golden brown. Drain on paper towels.  Lightly fry tortillas; not too crisp. To serve, place fried fish in a  tortilla, and top with shredded cabbage, and white sauce.
</span></li></ol>
